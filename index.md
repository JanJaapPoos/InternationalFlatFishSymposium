The International Flatfish Symposium is a long-running scientific conference dedicated to research on flatfish ecology, fisheries, aquaculture, and marine ecosystem dynamics. First established in the early 1990s following discussions at the 1989 European Marine Biology Symposium, the meeting has grown into one of the leading international forums for scientists working on species such as plaice, sole, flounder, halibut, and turbot. The symposium provides a platform where researchers, fisheries managers, and early-career scientists can exchange ideas, present new findings, and discuss emerging challenges affecting flatfish populations worldwide.

Over the years, the symposium has played an important role in shaping international collaboration in flatfish research. Meetings are intentionally kept relatively small to encourage discussion and active participation, and many contributions are later published in special issues of the Journal of Sea Research. The conference has been hosted in countries across Europe, North America, and Asia, reflecting the global importance of flatfish species for both ecosystems and fisheries. By fostering collaboration between researchers from different disciplines and regions, the International Flatfish Symposium continues to contribute to advances in sustainable fisheries management and marine conservation.

The 2024 edition of the symposium was held in Wageningen and organized by Wageningen University & Research. The event focused on a wide range of themes, including environmental drivers of flatfish populations, spatial behaviour and movement ecology, fisheries management, aquaculture and stock enhancement, and the effects of human activities such as trawling and offshore wind farm development. A distinctive feature of the symposium is its interdisciplinary nature, bringing together expertise from physiology, ecology, oceanography, socio-economics, and fisheries science to better understand the role of flatfish in marine ecosystems.
